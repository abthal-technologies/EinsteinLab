Physics of Vibration

1. Vibration in AIR (Light Damping)

In air, the resistive forces (air friction) are negligible. This allows the system to build up massive energy during Resonance. If the Drive Frequency matches the Natural Frequency exactly ($f = f_0$), the amplitude increases until the glass exceeds its elastic limit and shatters.

2. Vibration in WATER (Heavy Damping)

When submerged in water, the high viscosity creates Heavy Damping. The resistive force $F = -bv$ converts mechanical energy into heat instantly.
Key Result: The resonance curve becomes "Broad/Flat," and the amplitude is too small to shatter the glass.

3. Natural Frequency Shift

Notice that switching to water lowers the $f_0$. This is because the water surrounding the glass acts as "Added Mass." Since frequency is inversely proportional to the square root of mass ($f \propto 1/\sqrt{m}$), increasing mass decreases the natural frequency.
